Title: Feynman Proposal

Richard Feynman proposed the concept of quantum computing in 1982 during a conference at MIT.
He argued that classical computers cannot efficiently simulate quantum systems due to exponential complexity.

Feynman suggested that quantum systems should be simulated using quantum machines.
This idea laid the foundation for quantum computing.

He emphasized that quantum mechanics allows new computational possibilities through superposition and entanglement.
Feynman's proposal is considered the starting point of quantum computing research.
