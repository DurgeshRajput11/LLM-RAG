Title: Shor's Algorithm

Shor's algorithm was introduced by Peter Shor in 1994.
It is a quantum algorithm that efficiently factors large integers.

The algorithm runs exponentially faster than the best known classical algorithms for factoring.
This has major implications for cryptography, especially RSA encryption.

Shor's algorithm demonstrated that quantum computers could solve real-world problems more efficiently than classical computers.
It is one of the most important breakthroughs in quantum computing.
