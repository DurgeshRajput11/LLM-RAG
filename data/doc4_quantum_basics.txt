Title: Quantum Computing Basics

Quantum computing is based on principles of quantum mechanics such as superposition and entanglement.

Superposition allows a quantum bit, or qubit, to exist in multiple states simultaneously.
Unlike classical bits, which are either 0 or 1, qubits can be both at the same time.

Entanglement is a phenomenon where two qubits become linked and the state of one affects the other instantly.
These properties allow quantum computers to perform complex computations efficiently.

Quantum computing differs fundamentally from classical computing in how information is processed.
